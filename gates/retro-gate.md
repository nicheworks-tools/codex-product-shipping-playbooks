# Retro Gate

Use this gate to decide whether a retrospective is complete enough to be useful later.

## Pass conditions

- [ ] The retro summarizes the shipped change clearly
- [ ] What went well is recorded
- [ ] What went poorly is recorded
- [ ] At least one concrete improvement action exists if the retro is warranted
- [ ] The notes are practical enough to help future work
- [ ] The retro avoids vague or purely emotional language

## Questions to ask

- Would this help the team do the next similar change better?
- Are the lessons concrete enough to reuse?
- Did we record improvement actions instead of only observations?
- Is the retro short and useful rather than broad and unfocused?

## Fail conditions

Do not pass this gate if:

- the retro is mostly generic reflection
- improvement actions are missing
- the summary is too vague to be useful later
- the retro adds little beyond the existing diff review

## Output expected before passing

The following should exist in some usable form:

- retro summary
- what went well
- what went poorly
- improvement actions
